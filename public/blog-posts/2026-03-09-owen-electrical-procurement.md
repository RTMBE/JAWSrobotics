Owen, a key member of our electrical team, has been making excellent progress on sourcing the critical electrical components we need for our swerve drive robot. This procurement work is essential for staying on budget while ensuring we have high-quality, reliable parts.

**WAGO Connectors Acquired:**

Owen successfully obtained WAGO connectors for our robot's electrical system. These professional-grade wire connectors are a significant upgrade over traditional wire nuts or solder joints, offering several advantages:

• **Tool-Free Installation:** Quick and easy to connect and disconnect wires
• **Reliable Connections:** Spring-loaded contacts ensure consistent electrical contact
• **Vibration Resistant:** Critical for robotics applications where movement and impacts are constant
• **Reusable:** Can be reconfigured as our design evolves
• **Professional Standard:** Used in industrial applications, giving us competition-grade reliability

**S1 Motor Controller Research:**

Owen is currently researching and preparing an order for S1 motor controllers, which will be the brain of our swerve drive system. For a swerve drive robot, we need precise control over eight motors simultaneously:

• **4 Drive Motors:** Provide forward/backward motion for each wheel
• **4 Turning Motors:** Rotate each wheel module for omnidirectional movement

The S1 controllers offer the performance characteristics we need:
• High current capacity for our motors
• Precise speed and position control
• Compatibility with our planned control system
• Proven reliability in competitive robotics

**Wire and Cable Planning:**

In addition to the motor controllers, Owen is creating a comprehensive bill of materials for all the wiring we'll need. This includes:

• Power distribution cables (high current capacity for battery to controllers)
• Signal wires for sensors and communication
• Proper gauge selection for different current loads
• Color coding for easy troubleshooting and maintenance

**Budget-Conscious Approach:**

As high school students competing against professionals, we need to be smart about our spending. Owen is carefully comparing suppliers and specifications to get the best value without compromising on quality. Every dollar saved on components can be invested elsewhere in the robot.

**Next Steps:**

Once the S1 controllers arrive, Owen will work with Ben to begin testing the motor control system. We're building incrementally, testing each subsystem before integration, to ensure everything works reliably when we need it most—during competition.

Thank you to ODrive Robotics for making it possible for us to afford quality components and compete at this level!
