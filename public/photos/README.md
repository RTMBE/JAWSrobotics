# Photo Management Guide

This folder contains all the photos used on the JAWS Robotics website. The photos are organized into subfolders based on where they appear on the site.

## Folder Structure

- **home/** - Photos for the home page hero slider (3 photos recommended)
- **team/** - Team member photos for the About Us page (3 photos for Ben, Maksym, Owen)
- **cad/** - Robot CAD designs and build photos (12+ photos)
- **sponsors/** - Sponsor logos (ODRIVEBots and future sponsors)

## Team Logo

To add your JAWS Robotics logo:
1. Save your logo as `logo.png` or `logo.jpg`
2. Place it in the `public/` folder (not in the photos folder)
3. The header will automatically use it instead of the "J" placeholder

## How to Add Your Photos

### Step 1: Prepare Your Photos
- Use high-quality images (at least 1920x1080 for hero images)
- Optimize file sizes (compress images to reduce load times)
- Use JPG format for photos, PNG for logos with transparency

### Step 2: Name Your Files
Photos should be named sequentially:
- `photo1.jpg`, `photo2.jpg`, `photo3.jpg`, etc.

### Step 3: Place in Correct Folder
- Drop your photos into the appropriate subfolder
- The website will automatically display them

## Recommended Image Sizes

### Home Page Hero Images (home/)
- **Dimensions:** 1920x1080 or larger
- **Aspect Ratio:** 16:9
- **File Size:** Under 500KB each
- **Quantity:** 3 images (will rotate automatically)

### Team Photos (team/)
- **Dimensions:** 800x800 (square)
- **Aspect Ratio:** 1:1
- **File Size:** Under 200KB each
- **Quantity:** 8 or more

### CAD/Robot Photos (cad/)
- **Dimensions:** 1200x800 or larger
- **Aspect Ratio:** 3:2 or 16:9
- **File Size:** Under 300KB each
- **Quantity:** 12 or more

### Sponsor Logos (sponsors/)
- **Dimensions:** 600x300 (landscape) or 400x400 (square)
- **Format:** PNG with transparent background preferred
- **File Size:** Under 100KB each
- **Quantity:** As many as you have sponsors

## Tips for Best Results

1. **Use descriptive filenames** - While the system uses photo1.jpg, photo2.jpg, etc., you can rename them to be more descriptive if needed
2. **Compress images** - Use tools like TinyPNG or ImageOptim to reduce file sizes without losing quality
3. **Consistent aspect ratios** - Keep photos in the same category at similar aspect ratios for a clean look
4. **High quality** - Use the best quality photos you have - they represent your team!

## Placeholder Images

Until you add your own photos, the website uses placeholder images from Unsplash. Once you add your photos with the correct naming convention, they will automatically replace the placeholders.

## Need Help?

If you have questions about adding photos or need assistance, contact your web administrator.
